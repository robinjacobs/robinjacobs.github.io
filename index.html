Robin Jacobs

I'm a robotics engineer. In January 2024, I completed my Master of Science in Robotics, Systems and Control (RSC) from ETH Zurich. Currently, I am doing my civilian service in the Research in Orthopedic Computer Science Group (ROCS), where I assist in the development of camera-based surgical navigation systems.

I am broadly interested in the intersection of robotics and machine learning. I am particularly excited about how to combine novel learning-based methods for computer vision and control to enable robots to interact with the world in a more intelligent and autonomous way. This necessitates the need for safe and robust algorithms that can be deployed on real-world systems.

Using Reinforcement Learning For Grasping Under Uncertainty

Master Thesis, RSL ETH Zurich 2023

The project focuses on using reinforcement learning and force sensor measurements to improve the robustness of grasping objects in the presence of uncertainties. We explore the use of privileged teacher student learning and recurrent neural networks to encode belief states of the object's position. We deploy the learned policies to a real-world manipulation plattform and highlight the benefits of using force-based measurements to improve grasping success rates in the presence of estimation biases.

Approximate Predictive Control Barrier Function

Semester Project, IDSC ETH Zurich 2022

A predictive control barrier function (PCBF) based safety filter is a modular framework to verify safety of a control input by predicting a future trajectory. This work presents an approximation procedure that uses a neural network to approximate the optimal value function of the first optimization problem, which defines a control barrier function (CBF). With it we can ensure safety of inputs coming for example from a reinforcementlearning policy. The method is computationally cheap and can hence be deployed on real-time systems.
